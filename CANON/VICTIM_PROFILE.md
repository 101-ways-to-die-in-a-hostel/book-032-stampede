# Victim Profile

## Basic Information

**Full Name:** Marcus "Big Marc" Okonkwo
**Nickname/Handle:** @BigMarcTravels
**Age:** 24
**Nationality:** American (Nigerian heritage)
**Occupation:** Recent college graduate, aspiring sports blogger

## Archetype
**Primary:** Party Animal

**Secondary traits:** Social media obsessive, sports fanatic, natural entertainer who needs an audience

## The Fatal Flaw
**Core Flaw:** Recklessness

**How it manifests:**
- Always pushes to the front of crowds for content
- Ignores capacity limits and safety concerns for "the experience"
- Believes his size (6'3", 220 lbs) makes him invincible
- Prioritizes documenting moments over experiencing (or surviving) them

**How it kills them:**
His need to be at the center of the action places him in the worst possible position when panic strikes. His size, usually an advantage, becomes a liability when he falls and can't get back up against the crowd flow.

## Physical Appearance
- **Height/Build:** 6'3", 220 lbs, former college linebacker build going slightly soft
- **Hair:** Tight fade, always fresh - found a barber in every city
- **Distinguishing features:** Big, infectious smile; University of Maryland ring he never removes; tribal sleeve tattoo on left arm
- **Typical outfit:** American sports jerseys (owns one for every country he visits), basketball shorts, expensive sneakers he shouldn't be backpacking in

## Personality

### Positive Traits (that get twisted)
1. Infectious enthusiasm - becomes inability to read dangerous situations
2. Natural leadership - becomes dangerous encouragement of risky behavior

### Negative Traits (that seal their fate)
1. Clout-chasing - leads to positioning himself in dangerous crowd spots
2. Overconfidence in physical abilities - leads to underestimating crowd force

### Pet Peeves
- People who don't support their teams
- Quiet hostels with no atmosphere
- Being told spaces are "full"

### Fears
- Missing out on viral moments
- Being forgotten or irrelevant
- Silence and solitude

## Background

### Origin Story
Grew up in Silver Spring, Maryland, in a tight-knit Nigerian-American community. Star linebacker in high school, decent at University of Maryland before a knee injury ended his NFL dreams. Parents are disappointed he's traveling instead of getting a "real job." Started the travel blog to prove he could turn his personality into a career.

### Why They Travel
Chasing content and clout. Genuinely loves sports and wants to experience fan culture globally. Also running from the reality that his athletic career ended and he has no backup plan. The blog is his desperate bet on himself.

### How They Ended Up at This Hostel
Argentina vs. France World Cup final screening was advertised across all backpacker social media. This hostel in Buenos Aires promised "the most epic viewing party in South America." Marcus booked months in advance.

## Voice & Speech

### Vocabulary
- Uses words like: "lit," "bro," "no cap," "fire," "viral," "content," "goated"
- Avoids words like: "dangerous," "maybe not," "let's wait"
- Catchphrases: "You gotta be here for this!" "This is going to blow up!" "Trust the process!"

### Speech Pattern
- Loud and expressive, projects voice naturally
- Lots of sports metaphors and references
- Code-switches between AAVE with friends and "professional" voice for content
- Frequently addresses imaginary audience even when not filming

### Internal Monologue Style
Thinks in content ideas - constantly framing experiences as posts, videos, engagement opportunities. Underlying anxiety about relevance masked by bravado.

## Digital Presence

### Social Media
- **Platform of choice:** TikTok and Instagram Reels
- **Follower count:** 47,000 (grew from 2,000 during this trip)
- **Posting style:** High-energy reaction videos, "POV: American at [foreign sports event]" content
- **Last post before death:** Instagram story from the crowded common room: "THIS ENERGY IS CRAZY!! Argentina about to make history! #WorldCup #BuenosAires #VivaArgentina"

### Online vs Reality
Online: Successful travel blogger living his best life, surrounded by friends
Reality: Desperately needs validation, often alone between events, measures self-worth in views

## Relationships

### Family
- Parents in Maryland constantly asking when he'll "get serious"
- Younger sister is his biggest fan and most frequent commenter

### Friends
- College friends scattered, sees them less each year
- "Friends" are other content creators he collaborates with strategically

### Romantic
- Nothing serious, uses dating apps in each city
- Had a girlfriend in college who wanted him to grow up

### At the Hostel
- Immediately became the social center of any room
- Leading chants, buying rounds (putting it on credit cards)
- Other travelers either love his energy or find him exhausting

## The Day They Die

### Morning
Woke up hungover from the pre-game party the night before. Filmed a "getting ready for the biggest game ever" video. Put on his custom Argentina-US mashup jersey.

### Their Last Normal Moment
Standing near the front of the packed common room, arm around strangers, leading a chant as the game kicks off. Genuinely happy, feeling like he belongs.

### Final Thoughts
Confusion as the crowd suddenly shifts. Realization that he can't breathe. The desperate understanding that his size means nothing against thousands of pounds of human pressure.

### Last Words
"BRO, CHILL! CHILL!" (Screamed before being pulled under)

## Writing This Character

### POV Notes
When in this character's head:
- They notice: Energy levels, camera angles, "content moments," crowd excitement
- They ignore: Exit locations, capacity concerns, subtle shifts in crowd density
- They judge: "Boring" travelers who don't want to party, people who prioritize safety over experience

### Key Scenes Checklist
- [ ] Introduction scene establishing archetype - Marcus commanding the hostel bar on arrival
- [ ] Fatal flaw demonstration (early) - pushing past capacity limits at a previous event
- [ ] Sympathetic moment (make reader care) - video call with his sister, vulnerable about pressure to succeed
- [ ] Warning ignored scene - hostel staff nervously noting overcrowding, Marcus dismissing it
- [ ] Point of no return - the moment panic starts and he realizes he can't move
- [ ] The death
